This document addresses common questions from governments,

---

## Is TEOS Forge tied to any national government?

No.

TEOS Forge originated in Egypt but is **not controlled by any government**.
It is designed as a **sovereign, jurisdiction-neutral framework**
that can be adopted independently by any institution.

---

## Does adopting TEOS Forge affect national sovereignty?

No.

Adopting institutions retain full sovereignty over:
- Governance decisions
- Legal compliance
- Repositories and data
- Treasury or funding mechanisms (if enabled)

TEOS Forge provides structure, not control.

---

## Can an institution exit after adopting TEOS Forge?

Yes.

Exit is an explicit design principle.

Institutions may:
- Fork repositories
- Modify governance locally
- Discontinue use without penalty

All governance and audit records remain accessible.

---

## Is there any vendor lock-in?

No.

TEOS Forge is:
- Open by default
- Vendor-neutral
- Platform-agnostic

No proprietary dependencies are required.

---

## How does auditability work?

Auditability is continuous and documented.

Key elements include:
- Immutable audit logs
- Explicit decision records
- Reviewable governance actions
- Separation of powers

Audit does not depend on trust in individuals.

---

## Does TEOS Forge require blockchain or tokens?

No.

Blockchain and on-chain execution are **optional extensions**.
The core framework operates fully off-chain.

Institutions may adopt governance and audit
without any cryptographic or financial components.

---

## Can TEOS Forge be adapted to local law and policy?

Yes.

The framework is designed to be:
- Legally adaptable
- Policy-aligned
- Configurable per jurisdiction

Local governance documents may supersede defaults.

---

## Who controls updates to TEOS Forge itself?

The public TEOS Forge reference implementation
is governed transparently.

Adopting institutions are never required
to accept upstream changes.

---

## What kinds of systems is TEOS Forge suitable for?

TEOS Forge is suitable for:

- National digital platforms
- Identity, registry, or service infrastructure
- Shared public software foundations
- Cross-agency or cross-border DPI initiatives

It is not designed for short-lived or speculative projects.
